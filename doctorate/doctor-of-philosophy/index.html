The Doctor of Philosophy

The Doctor of Philosophy (PhD) is a three- to seven-year advanced academic research degree designed for scholars, researchers, and professionals who aim to generate original knowledge and contribute significantly to their field. The programme develops expertise in theoretical analysis, critical inquiry, and rigorous research methodologies. Unlike professional doctorates such as the EdD, the PhD emphasises the creation of new scholarly knowledge and prepares graduates for careers in academia, research-intensive roles, and specialised leadership positions across sectors.

Students work closely with expert supervisors, engage in advanced research training, and produce a substantial doctoral thesis that demonstrates independent, original contribution to knowledge.

Who Is This Programme For?

This PhD programme is ideal for:

  • Master’s degree holders seeking to advance into academic or research careers.
  • Researchers or analysts aiming to deepen expertise in theory, methodology, and scholarly inquiry.
  • Professionals wishing to contribute new knowledge to their discipline through advanced research.
  • University lecturers and academics pursuing career progression, research productivity, or professorial pathways.
  • Leaders, consultants, and policymakers seeking to develop expertise grounded in evidence and original scholarship.

Learning Outcomes

By the end of the PhD programme, candidates will be able to:

  • Demonstrate mastery of conceptual, theoretical, and research knowledge within their field.
  • Design and execute independent, original research that contributes new insights to scholarship.
  • Critically evaluate existing research, theories, and methodologies.
  • Apply advanced analytical techniques to complex datasets and research problems.
  • Publish research outcomes in academic journals and conferences.
  • Communicate complex ideas clearly to specialist and non-specialist audiences.
  • Advance into academic, research, and senior leadership roles globally.

Academic Requirements

Applicants must meet one of the following entry criteria:

  • A Master’s degree in a relevant field (Merit/Distinction preferred).
  • An international qualification equivalent to a UK master’s degree.
  • Exceptional candidates with a strong First-Class bachelor’s degree (subject to approval).
  • Evidence of strong research potential or professional experience relating to the proposed area of research.

Additional requirements:

  • Official academic transcripts.
  • Detailed research proposal or statement of research interests.
  • Academic or professional references.
  • Updated CV highlighting research or professional experience.

English Language Requirements

Students must provide one of the following:

  • IELTS Academic 6.5 (minimum 6.0 in each band).
  • TOEFL iBT 80.
  • PTE Academic 58.
  • LAMS Placement Test.
  • Proof of previous study in English (subject to verification).
